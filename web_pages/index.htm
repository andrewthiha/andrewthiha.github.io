PACE Race 5K

The 2nd Annual Event
By Kathy Freedman
October 30, 2004
Washington, DC
For the Washington Running Report



At 9:00 on Saturday morning (October 30, 2004), more than 300 runners took off down Pennsylvania Avenue toward a fog-shrouded Capitol. The 2nd annual PACE Race 5K came to Washington, DC the last weekend in October as part of its nation-wide circuit. The events raise awareness and funds for research for prostate cancer.

In the men's race, 23-year-old Thomas Weiler of Washington, DC (photo) led the field home in a time of 16:18. Ten seconds back was Pascal Brazey, 35, also of Washington, DC. McLean's Ted Poulos, 42, took third place and honors as the first masters division finisher.





In the women's race, Amy Lestrange, 24, of Washington, DC (photo), led the competition by 15 seconds, crossing the line in 18:32. Mary Davison traveled to DC from Chesapeake, Virginia, ran a 6:03 pace, and finished second. Third place went to Katie Goodwin, 25, of Washington, DC, for her time of 20:16 (6:32 pace). Betty Blank, 51, of Falls Church, Virginia, was the first masters division finisher for the women, placing fourth overall in 20:44.

Following the race, runners and walkers munched on fresh bagels, bananas, apples, Propel Fitness Water, and other goodies, and visited the "Ask the Doctor" table to obtain information about prostate cancer screening and prevention.



Photo above: Amy Beer and James Frenzel, both of Washington, DC, finished together at 23:14 and 23:15 respectively.
